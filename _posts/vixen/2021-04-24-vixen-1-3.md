Fuyuka was ready to fall to her knees in frustration. She could not expect to be invited to an uninhabited shrine.


"Is this what they call _yukai-han_...?"[^1]


The scenery is resplendent in its architecture, however, in spite of the holiday, say nothing of worshippers and tourists, not even Shinto priests or other shrine workers might be spotted. This fact plainly apprised her that something as an exorcism is not practised  thereat.<!--excerpt-->


"Seriously—, I've been deceived...? Aha-ha..."


She may but sneer at so pathetic a state of her own, which was brought on by repeated so far ill fortune. Nevertheless, there was nothing she could do about it, therefore Fuyuka dutifully cleansed her hands, flung five yen into offertory box, and put her palms together.


(I won't aim too high, but I hope my luck will improve  at least slightly... Or rather, let me return to my normal life, please~...)


Since she has actually been  lured to this shrine, whether the prayer would be heard was questionable. However, rather than head home without doing a thing, she believed it was better to throw some monetary offer.


"Ha—, now home..."


She murmured so to herself, concluding her prayers and slumping her shoulders.
She cannot squander any more  of her precious holiday time. Even heading home now, she will be back only past noon, but she still seemingly will be able to  take a nap at the least.


Upon the very moment she thought so:


"Ah? Er, impossible."


In response to sensation of falling down upon her skin water droplets, Fuyuka reflexively raised her gaze up at the sky: Quietly, it began to rain.
The sky was cloudless, azure far and wide. Certainly, sun rays were also aptly reaching the earth. Realising that this is a phenomenon of sun shower, she dropped her shoulders in such disheartening as never before.


"The rain... just after my prayer... What's up with that."


For the weather forecast predicted a fine weather, and  no tendency for rain was there just before she left home, she brought no umbrella.
For the railway station was at reasonable distance, and for she did not wish to board the train all soaked, she could but await thereat.


"What the deuce~... I must be the most miserable in whole Japan..."


She seated herself down upon the roofed stairs leading to offertory box, then, at last, curling her knees, she lowered her head.


"I mean, honestly, how could I end up like this."


—Ding.<!--(──)-->


The vocabulary of resignation, which passed from her lips, was not addressing to mere  presently falling  sun shower.
But towards all the happenings from parting with the girl, whom she regarded dearly, to misfortunes at her work and daily life.


—Ding.


Within human luck there are passim its ups and downs indeed. To be human is not to ever experience nice events akin to residing in the middle of Kingdom of Heaven, however at times to taste a suffer of being as though at the very bed of the hell. In despite of fathoming that this is the true charm of life, Fuyuka cannot help feeling that only she was undergoing so constant a distress. This is also the thought into which continuously assaulted by misery human  eventually descends.


—Ding.


For this reason, even when upon times like these precisely something big and nice is also arrives...


—Ding-dong.


"Now, come on—! So noisy—! You've been ringing and ringing and ringing!"


Drowning amid her negative thought, Fuyuka could hear a beauteous bell-esque tone, which she would normally listen to, but as of today, feeling an innermost exasperation, she raised her visage and cried out.


"Eh?"


That countenance dyed in indignation, however, froze in an instant.


"Where, have this...?"


She have come to the designated shrine today—the Shinto shrine with large _torii_ gate, _chozuya_, and neat main building.


She _should_ have come, and yet, _whoosh_, the sight altered utterly.


In stead of large _torii_, series of small ones were set up, forming a tunnel; the environs caught her unawares by profound woodland; and the main shrine in rear have turned even larger and more austere than afore.


_Ching-a-ling_, ring out a sound. It was consimilar to sound of the bell earlier, this time, however, it has been loud enough to plainly remain in her ear; Fuyuka felt goose bumps forcibly arise, in the face of the warm weather.


"...Hh."


Fuyuka was so terrified that  lost her sound, and must have lost the ability of respiration, too, presuming carelessness. She was under the terror of one who fails to escape, being in a grip of apparition.


_Ching-a-ling_, sound echoed once anew. Considerably nearer this time, what enlightened her that it was drawing closer from the heart of the train of _torii_ gates.
But Fuyuka is unable to escape. In the first place, she was embedded in dense thicket, and the only path she could dash through had been the corridor of lined _torii_ gates.


Ching-a-ling!


Sound blared across the grounds of the shrine, making Fuyuka start and peer ahead. Then, _Eep_, she gasped.
Obviously, instrument would produce no sound unless someone rings it. She could realise that someone was making a bell-esque sound, but now, catching sight of the figure, Fuyuka felt like fleeing into the thicket.


It was a group. It tread slowly, lined, evenly spaced; the figures were clad in _miko_[^2] garments of basic tone of white; the faces all covered fox masks. Owing to which, it carried an otherworldly horror.


"E-e-ek..."


Intuition assures her not to get involved. Recalling horror stories and urban legends—wherein is often said if one were to follow such a group, they will be spirited away to the other world—the dread seized her all the farther.
Little by little the females in _miko_ garments was advancing forth, whereas chiming the _kagura suzu_[^3] which they bore in their hands. The rain had not ceased even as the space shifted, but none of the females have held up an umbrella.


"Um?"


No. Not none.
Amidst the group therein was opened but solitary umbrella with blooming red and white[^4] flowers. As females neared themselves, Fuyuka perceived that an umbrella was keeping  from rain enveloped into white _kimono_ maiden.
The maiden behind umbrella, although have had her visage obscured by silk floss headdress, was not of lofty stature. If she is not much mistaken, perhaps about a height of middle school pupil.
Whilst being absorbed by spectacle, the group proceed along the pathway upon the very centre,  until finally made its way to the main shrine's... offertory box, whereat, upon the stairs, was assumed Fuyuka.


"..."


As the females halted, _shin-shin_,[^5] the only noise which could reach the ear was the noise of the rain which kept outpouring itself. Fuyuka, of course, might afford no room to listen to this noise but await for  situation to develop, while stay petrified.
She had vague idea of the span of elapsed time, however as the group have formed a line before Fuyuka, the child clad in white _kimino_ has stepped forth. Who has been followed by sole priestess maintaining an umbrella.
As soon as the child in white _kimono_ walked up to Fuyuka, joining her feet together, she bowed politely and beautifully. Fuyuka was uncertain whether it was greeting, but unintentionally  nodded her head in the same fashion.


Thus, at last, Fuyuka was able to gaze upon the visage of the maiden in white _kimono_. Her eyes reflected round and big and clear pupils, along with small and soft and juicy as cherry lips. The features were predictably that of a child, however appearance of the sweet smile, _doki_, sent a shiver though Fuyuka's heart.


(Wh, what!? What should I do!?)


Not before she began to wonder "What in the world is going on, might this be some queer dream," the voice of the child in white _kimono_ gently filled the air.


"Nice to meet you. I am Ohana."
"...Yes?"
"I've come here as your bride, my Lady. And although I may be inexperienced, I look forward to spend with you many a years to come."
