"I'm sorry. I enjoyed a time with you, Fuyuka, still I have a family of my own."
"...Ha?"


Fuyuka was relatively mediocre woman, merely when it comes to her outlook upon love she inclined to prefer the same sex. Within her mind, she may say with confidence that her favourite partner is a girl; within general society, however, that would be  called a homosexual.
Albeit her romantic feelings have become accepted considerably often, weighting with the past, Fuyuka has hitherto not a slightest intention of disclosing them to surroundings.


The girl she met in the midst of all this was an irreplaceable presence for Fuyuka, who will turn twenty-five this year. And yet, smiling  awkwardly upon apology, the girl was standing before her very eyes.


"W, wait. Family? What do you mean...? Er, you said you was single."
"Uh... It was difficult for me to tell you, too. I'm sorry."
"You... Th, then everything until now—"
"Anyhow, I don't think we will be meeting anymore. Well then..."
"Er, eh—???"


The parting was all too sudden.
She chanced to find a kindred spirit at _izakaya_,[^1] whereupon they developed a relationship over the curse of their association. However, Fuyuka could not imagine that the girl was married.


From that event, the dark age looked in upon Fuyuka.


She does not feel reluctant of being gay, however finding the right companion is by no means easy. Not just there are high hurdles to overcome, she  has also to ascertain  that  both her partner and herself may accept each other's feelings.


It was only natural that Fuyuka, being released into this severe environment, will be unable to find a replacement for her immediately; and it was upon that time when bad things began to occur in a rapid succession.
From unassuming to great misfortunes: In terms  of work, she frequently makes trivial mistakes, and, incurring her superior's wrath, in due course, finds herself moping up the blunders of her subordinates—by and large, she has a parade of struggles.
Apart from work, beginning with the trifle of  broken heel, getting splashed by car, belating to work for delay of the train, acute breakage of a home appliances at her quarters, it goes on and on—she suffered more misfortunes that could possibly list.


"Ha-a-a—."


Those days were abrading spirits of Fuyuka, and her stride was heavy as she withdrew from the office at the end of her overtime.
It is past nine o'clock, however the train is brimming with passengers. Fuyuka also joins the suited figures  of both sexes, who is worn-out from overtimes, _yura-yura_,[^2] lurching along with them. Having no energy to draw her smart phone, she was gripping her strap to let the time pass.


About fifteen-minutes' walk separates the railway station and her lonely apartment. With no energy to head to the supermarket, she bought an adequate _bento_ at convenience store and goes upon her way home. It was terribly desolate​ retreating figure of twenty-five-year-old company employee.


(I wonder for how long will this luck stay with me.)


When the girl had hitherto been with her, she was somehow able to handle the hardships, presently, however, being stripped of this support, all it takes is a mere gust of wind and she is about to collapse.


"Thank you very much."


As Fuyuka tottered before the station, the hand distributing a leaflets was extended to her from alongside.  She accepted it almost subconsciously. She is one of those people who tends to take tissues and leaflets all the time.


"...Ahn?"


Most leaflets at these occasions are but advertisements for facilities nigh the station, such as sports gyms, restaurants, and the likes. Nevertheless, looking at it under the casual light of street lamp, she found an unexpected characters drawn upon it:


"For those of you who have nothing but misfortunes! Why don't you exorcise it? Free First Time Service!"
"..."


Fuyuka frowned at this awfully cheap culmination. For an instant, she thought "To exorcise it is a good notion, isn't it," however the content of writing was horrible.


(First time is free, eh... We're not in that kind of service industry.)


The leaflet contained a phrase, a picture of Shinto shrine, and then a telephone number. Shrine appeared to be located in mountains, a little ways away from here, is not too far, however, to reach by train.
She do not intend to start off, but exorcism might be a good thought. For present Fuyuka seems to be favoured by bad luck. Casually, she stole a glance back at the spot where the person who furnished her with this leaflet had been.


"...Eh?"


There were none such, however, but people wandering about after day at the office.

Saturday, several days later. Taking advantage of her day off, amidst the mountains, Fuyuka was ascending the stairway.


"I wonder if it was too easy..."


Wearing a jacket over the shirt and jeans at the bottom for moving easier, Fuyuka strode her way through the stairs.
Back then, returning home, after worrying herself sick, she called the number. She also had considered a probability of fraud, however she could hang up at once if so. In fact, she might have been mentally cornered to the point where she had to resort to it.<!--???-->
She was rather nervous during a call, but a clerical voice of female had answered, and in no time she had been appointed a date and time.


"I'm dubious, but... I have no choice but to rely on this, have I, as of now."


She switched her day off of sleeping as dead with an apotropaic magic, in hope to see some light and keep moving forth.
Fuyuka had no minute knowledge of Shinto shrines, however upon arrival to one she could not help a cry of admiration.


"Amazing, so handsome shrine in the middle of nowhere..."


She was greeted by large _torii_[^3] gate, then main shrine building, not losing in size, and well-built _chozuya_[^4] with a pure water leisurely streaming through. At first glance, it had been an excellent shrine doubtless. It even seemed to bear this beneficial aura.


"Wh, why?"


Nevertheless, finally reaching there and minutely surveying surroundings, Fuyuka's lips  quivered. The reason was plain and simple.


"Why is no one here—!"
