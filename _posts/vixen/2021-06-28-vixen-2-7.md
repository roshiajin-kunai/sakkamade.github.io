"Pray excuse me, excuse me, excuse me!"
"No need to be so apologetic... I didn't get up either."


_Peko-peko_,[^1] there stay Ohana, apologising. It was dark outside by now, and the point of prolonged slumber was evident. Fuyuka has not been anxious to put the blame upon the girl, for she herself shared the position.


"B-but, if I start preparing it now..."


In despite of feeling a light tightening of the chest at Ohana, who brought her tail frontwards and was embracing it by her arms, Fuyuka was reasoning as of their further actions.
As Ohana say, her cooking is fairly laborious, it takes time. Presuming she had started readying at this point, there is no doubt that it bound to reach completion only by reasonably late hour.


"Hmm—."


The food they bought is not something quick to spoil, it is okay to stuff it into the fridge. Admitting that, "Would it be fine to go to buy another _bento_ today," as Fuyuka had mulled over a variety of things in her head, an electronic piping resounded in her ear.


"Oh, you cook the rice..."
"I thought I could at least cook the rice beforehand, the reservation."<!--???-->
"You thought so. We have a rice, then..."


"So witty she is, or rather, she had prepared so much without my knowledge," was slightly impressed Fuyuka; however, owing to that, an excellent idea emerged in her mind.


"_Yosh_. I will arrange the things today!"
"Eh? Eeh!?"


Ohana could but produce a cry of wonder​ to such curiously enthusiastic Fuyuka.


♢


After all, it was not that Fuyuka could not cook. It was only that she had lost the time to do it.
Well, on the other hand, she is not so well versed in it as Ohana is, her forte are so simple as roasting and frying courses, and her best dishes are curry and something in that vicinity.


Hence, what she is at attempt to make this time is likewise...


"Um, what exactly are you making?"
"Fu-fu-fu, as long as I have rice, eggs and pasta, I can make anything."


Of course, she cannot possibly make _anything_. It is said to be the simplest dish to prepare in hectic working life.


"So, today, I'm making _chahan_[^2] and pasta."
"_Chahan_? Pasta?"


That was not of an elaborate cuisine. To put it bluntly, _chahan_ is prepared merely by frying eggs with cooked rice and then pouring the sauce over it, while pasta is nothing but a boiled noodles and sauce poured on top;<!--???--><!--???--> the cooking may be arranged at a very short notice.
...Although whether to call it cooking is a delicate matter.


"I never knew these things came in handy! Sure a lot to learn."
"I doubt you'll learn anything new here... This is for people who are short on time."


Upon condition of not doing anything too untoward, making it delicious is elementary, what was welcome for busy Fuyuka; albeit eating it every day would  grow tiresome, it was cheap and a strong ally for those living alone.


"_Yosh_, it's done!"


She wondered "How this and self-satisfied look of mine can be compared to Ohana's cookery," but it certainly is pretty remarkable as far as cost and time are concerned.


"Let's get over with this for today. You'll have to swing your arm[^3] tomorrow."
"Uugh, excuse me..."


Ohana seemed apologetic the whole time. Ohana appears to bear a keen sense of responsibility, being profoundly distressed, even though she is in no way the one at fault.


"You done nothing wrong, Ohana. You must have had a tiring day, and falling asleep after it is normal."
"None the less, after we went through all the trouble of buying the ingredients..."
"You may use them tomorrow. You don't have to be bothered by it, hence."
"And yet...  still, I am your wife."
"I've been wondering of it for a while, but... this sense of value of yours, it's fairly outmoded, isn't it, Ohana?"
"He?"
"Gender and marital equality are norm in this day and age, after all. Well, not everyone thinks like that."


Above all, therein a precondition of accepting Ohana as a wife, too, however she set to lay that issue aside now.


"I feel I've told you before, but you don't have to carry all the household chores on yourself, alright? If anything, I feel as though I'm depended on you too much, and I, on the contrary, hate it."
"Is that... so?"
"So it is. I deem we must assign us our roles if we can."


Although for Fuyuka, a working member of society, it is arduous to carry them during weekdays. As such...


"Why don't I take care of the housework on my days off?"


If the girl is responsible for housework for five days a week, there is nothing wrong in her doing it for some two holidays. It may seem rather unequal, however this is best present Fuyuka may offer.


"Th, this much... shall be fine?"
"Good! It's not as if I will do everything. Besides, in the end, I entirely entrusting it to you for all weekdays."
"That is perfectly fine, however... and meals shall also be on you, Fuyuka-san?"
"Eh..."


The word "housework" is also implies the kitchen work. The way Fuyuka put it, she bound to handle that part of business too...


(...Meal... Meal?)


What crosses Fuyuka's mind are dishes which cooked by Ohana so far. Whereupon, what breaks into her sight is a modest cooking which she made herself.


"......I, may I ask you to please do the meals?"
"Y-yes! Pray let me manage them!"


Her initial resolution was nowhere to be found, and in exceedingly feeble voice, Fuyuka bowed her head. Unfortunately, Ohana seemed to have already taken her stomach in a firm grip.
